Franklin Park Conservatory and Botanical Gardens in Columbus Ohio

Franklin Park Conservatory and Botanical Gardens is a classy landmark and attraction featuring high quality plant collections form around the globe, eye pleasing gardens indoor and outdoor, they have local seasonal art,shopes and most exhibits are nature-based,for visitors of all ages.It is set on 88 acre public park apx 2 miles east of downtown Columbus.


Franklin Park Conservatory and Botanical Gardens
1777 E. Broad St., Columbus, OH 43203
(614) 715-8000
www.fpconservatory.org

 

The Chicago's World Fair in 1890 help to inspired to have build the Victorian style Palm House in Columbus. Today known as John F. Wolfe Palm House is still drawing in large crowds. The Glass structured house is more than 100 years old.

The masterpiece of Franklin Park holds apx. forty-three types of palms from all around the globe, some endangered and much threatened in the wild.
